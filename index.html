Titles

The title is the most important part of any web page. Keep this in mind when writing your page title and make sure that it's relevant to your content. Page titles should be specific, keyword-rich, and under 70 characters (including spaces and punctuation). If the title is too long, it will get cut off in SERPs (Search Engine Results Pages).

It's also a good idea if the title ends in the name of the site. For example:

Page Title | Your Site Name

Headers

Headers are the second-most important part of any web page. They help organize the content by giving quick information to readers to know whether or not they should read the text in the section preceding it. If you ever feel tempted to bold, underline, or italicize content at the beginning of a new part of content, you should choose to use a header instead

Headers should be used in order. For example:

  1. H1: Similar or the same to the title of your page. Only use this ONCE per page.
  2. H2: Second sub-set of content. Should also sparingly.
  3. H3: Smaller headings for more nitty-gritty content. Can be used as often as needed.
  4. H4: Just like H3 but should be nestled within H3's.

Special note: screen readers use headers to help break content up for the visually impaired or users who are using screen readers for other reasons.

Meta Descriptions

If this is new to you, that's okay! A meta description is a short paragraph used to quickly describe the page to a screen reader and SERP. It is not shown directly on the page to the end user. However, it's still extremely valuable to have. Lastly, it's also the text that is picked up by social media sites such as Facebook and Twitter when you share a link.

Meta descriptions must be 156 characters (including spaces and punctuation) or else it will get cut off in the SERP. A good rule of thumb is to end the paragraph with a strong call to action, such as "Learn more!" For example:

Join us for the Fall Festival Sale from October 20, 2018 until October 31, 2018 where you'll get 50% bonus savings! Learn more!

Images

You may not initially think that images are important for SEO. However, in recent years, search engines have been heavily indexing images since Image Search is so heavily used now. Therefore, it's important to properly label your images.

All images require an "alt text" (stands for alternative text), which identifies the images to screen readers and SERPs. Also, if for some reason your image does not load properly for the end user, it will show them the alt text so they know what was intended to be there. Here's an example of how the code works in HTML:

img src="url" alt="descriptive text"
  • Keep images less than 100k in size. This will keep loading times faster and more friendly for mobile users.
  • Always use an alt text for an image.
  • Don't use other people's images. This could be a copyright violation.

Keywords

Last but not least, it's important to plan out your keywords for any article or web page you write.

Think about the exact words and phrases that your users may use to look up information on your page. Are they looking for glasses for eyes or for beer? If you know that they use multiple words for the same thing, be sure to sprinkle those words in throughout the text as variations. Then, make sure these are reflected in the title, headers, and meta description as well. Examples:

  • Beer Glasses
  • Glasses for Beer
  • Beer in a Glass
  • Beer Steins
  • Glassware for Beer
